Key Aspects of Mobile Design

Importance of Designing for
Mobile Devices

Average Irish person spends 4.5 hours on their mobile every day

  • For context, the average person sleeps for 8 hours,
    meaning they spend almost 30% of their waking day
    on their phone.

  • Many large emerging markets are 'Mobile-first countries', examples of such would be India, Indonesia and South Korea. Due to this substantial demographic, it is very important to have a user friendly and fluid design on Mobile devices.

  • If your service is not well designed in terms of mobile usage, users will get frustrated and stop using it.

Minimise Cognitive Load

Declutter

  • Remove anything that is not necessary

  • Keep interface elements to a minimum

  • Use progressive disclosure

Put the User in Control

Keep interactive elements familiar and predictable

  • Mobile users can't hover over an element the way PC users can

  • Users need to understand what went wrong and how to fix it.

  • Error messages should be in simple, concise language

Optimise Content for Mobile

Make it readable and legible

  • Users need to understand what went wrong and how to fix it.

  • Error messages should be in simple, concise language

  • Contrast between text and background
